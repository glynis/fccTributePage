Dennis Peron & Jonathan West

Fathers of the Medical Marijuana Movement

Image from the Act Up Archives.

  • 1945 - Dennis Peron born in New York City.
  • 1959 - Jonathan West born in Texas.
  • 1967 - Dennis moves to San Francisco en route to Vietnam. He served in the Air Force.
  • 1968 - Dennis' unit is intercepted and held in conflict for a week outside Saigon. Dennis is tasked with stacking the body bags. The experience leads to his decision to work as an activist for peace.
  • 1969 - Dennis returns to San Francisco from Vietnam with two pounds of marijuana in a duffle bag, leaves the Air Force, and comes out as a gay man.
  • 1970 - The San Francisco Police Department's Narcotics Division busts Dennis' marijunana business for the first of many times. Dennis immediately resumes selling marijuana out of his living room, stating his belief that no one could tell him he didn't have the right to smoke and obtain marijuana.
  • 1974 - Dennis opens a restaurant called "The Island" in the Castro District of San Francisco. Smoking and buying marijuana is allowed on the restaurant's second floor.
  • 1975 - Harvey Milk uses The Island as campaign headquarters during his unsuccessful run for State Assembly.
  • 1976 - The first case of HIV is diagnosed in San Francisco.
  • 1977 - California reduces penalties for posession of marijuana from a felony to a misdemeanor, statewide.
  • 1978 - The SFPD Narcotics Division once again raid Dennis' home and business, then known as "The Big Top." During the raid, Dennis is shot in the thigh. At his trial, the officer who shot Dennis admitted to attempted murder, saying if he had succeeded it would have been "one less faggot in San Francsico." This admission of an intended hate crime helps reduce Dennis' sentence.
  • 1989, December - Dennis and his long-term life-partner Jonathan are living together in San Francisco. Jonathan is dying of AIDS. The disease has left him covered in Kaposi’s Sarcoma lesions and the medication makes him too nauseated to eat. He weighs only 90 pounds. Marijuana is the only thing that helps him to eat, sleep, and feel relief from pain.
  • 1990, January - Dennis and Jonathan's apartment is raided by the SFPD Narcotics Division. Jonathan was in possession of four ounces of marijuana. The police take Jonathan and the evidence outside, beating and threatening the already weak Jonathan with a gun before putting him in the cruiser. Dennis watches from the top floor of their home.
  • 1990, August - Jonathan states in court that the marijuana was his, that he was using it medicinally to treat the symptoms of AIDS, and that Dennis was his caretaker.
  • 1990, September - Jonathan dies in San Francisco.
  • 1991 - Dennis organizes the successful Proposition P in San Francisco. While not leagally binding, it declares the citie's overwhelming support of medical marijuana.
  • 1992 - Dennis opens the San Francisco Cannabis Buyers Club, the first medical marijuana dispensary in the United States. It's legality is a gray area and the club is subject to scruitiny.
  • 1995 - The San Francisco Cannabis Buyers Club reaches 4,000 members, among them many major figures in marijuana activism including Dale Gieringer, current head of California NORML. Together they begin drafting Proposition 215.
  • 1996, June - The San Francisco Cannabis Buyers Club has over 90 employees, many of whom have AIDS and struggle to find non-discriminatory work places. 16 bakers working out of their own homes make enough to sell up to 500 baked goods per day. Dennis charges $5 per brownie, but if you were sick and broke, they were free. The club no longer allows prescription holders to bring guests, but all over 55 are welcome without question. Membership exceeds 10,000.
  • 1996, August - The California Bureau of Narcotics Enforcement deploys 100 agents to raid the Buyers Club. They take 150 pounds of marijuana, 400 plants, $60k in cash, and thousands of people's diagnosis letters. The San Franscisco Medical Society protests this confiscation as a violation of doctor-patient confidentiality. The CBNE begins a three month investigation of the club; while the doors were closed, several churches took up distrobution in its place.
  • 1996, September - Author of Doonesberry, Garry Trudeau, features a character saying, “I can’t get hold of any pot for our AIDS patients. Our regular sources have been spooked ever since the Cannabis Buyers’ Club in San Francisco got raided...” The strip is published in the SF Chronicle, LA Times, and many other California papers. The cartoon helped raise awareness and support for medical marijuana statewide.
  • 1996, November - Proposition 215 is passed as The Compassionate Care Act of California. The law allowed those with a written recommendation from a doctor to cultivate cannabis for personal medical use. The law made it to the ballot with the help of a Manhattan think tank now called the Drug Policy Center and George 'I Guarantee It' Zimmer, the Founder of Men's Warehouse. It is the first legal recognition of marijuana's medical potential in United States' history and the beginning of a domino effect, still in motion today.
  • 1998 - Empowered by the success of Proposition 215 in California, activists in Washington, Oregon, Alaska, Nevada, Maine, and Washington D.C. pass medical marijuana initiatives. D.C.'s initiative has ammendements added to slow its progress, but by 1999 the ammendments will be overturned.
  • 2000 - Hawai'i becomes the first state to legalize medical marijuana in state legislature. Colorado passes a medical marijuana initiative.
  • 2003 - California Senate Bill 420 passes, clarifying and slightly expanding the scope of the Compassionate Care Act to protect cooperative cultivation and transportation. Activist members of Dennis' Cannabis Buyers Club are instrumental in fighting against excessive regulation.
  • 2004 - Vermont legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature. Montanna legalizes by ballot measure.
  • 2006 - Rhode Island legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature.
  • 2007 - New Mexico legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature.
  • 2008 - Michigan legalizes medical marijuana by ballot measure.
  • 2010 - New Jersey legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature. Arizona legalizes by ballto measure.
  • 2011 - Delaware legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature.
  • 2012 - Connecticut legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature. Massachusetts legalizes by ballot measure.
  • 2013 - New Hampshire legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature.
  • 2014 - Maryland, Minnesota, and New York legalize medical marijuana in state legislature.
  • 2016 - Ohio and Pennsylvania legalize medical marijuana in state legislature. North Dakota, Arkansas, and Florida legalized by ballot measure.
  • 2017 - West Virginia legalizes medical marijuana in state legislature.

"The entire medical cannabis movement owes a direct debt to the gay community of San Francisco. It came out of the personal relationship of two gay men and the commitment of those people to each other. There's a very, very direct line of debt there."

— Steve DeAngelo, Executive Director of Oakland California's Harborside Health Center
